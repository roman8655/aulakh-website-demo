Couples Therapy

Compassionate therapy to strengthen connection, trust, and understanding.

Our therapists specialize in Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) — an evidence-based, attachment-focused approach. By helping partners understand both helpful and unhelpful dynamics, EFT supports the development of secure, responsive, and supportive relationships—the foundation of lasting love.

What is Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy?

Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) is one of the most researched and effective approaches to couples therapy. Developed by Dr. Sue Johnson and Dr. Les Greenberg, EFT is grounded in Attachment Theory, which emphasizes our natural need for closeness, comfort, and emotional security throughout life. Relationships can become strained when partners feel disconnected, unheard, or unsure of each other's availability. These moments often lead to cycles of conflict or distance, even when both partners long to feel close.

EFT helps couples:
  • Identify negative interaction cycles.
  • Understand the deeper emotions and unmet needs beneath conflict.
  • Express those needs in a safer, more open way.
  • Respond to each other with empathy, care, and emotional accessibility.

As couples learn to "listen with the heart," they begin to reconnect in ways that reduce defensiveness and strengthen emotional intimacy.

Research shows:
  • 70-75% of couples move from distress to recovery within 10-12 sessions.
  • 90% of couples experience meaningful improvement.

EFT is effective with couples facing a wide range of challenges, including trauma, depression, chronic stress, infidelity, and long-standing communication difficulties.


To learn more about EFT, visit the International Centre for Excellence in Emotionally Focused Therapy.

How EFT Helps Couples Reconnect

In EFT, we work with you to build a “safe haven” in your relationship—where both partners can feel secure, supported, and emotionally connected.

As this emotional safety grows:
  • Conflicts become easier to navigate.
  • Vulnerability becomes safer.
  • Communication feels clearer and less defensive.
  • Teamwork and collaboration improve.
  • Partners feel more understood and supported.

With strengthened connection and trust, couples are better able to handle challenges and sustain long-term closeness.

Theraputic Process

We use a structured, compassionate process to gain a clear and full understanding of your relationship dynamics.
Session 1 - Couples Session

Meet together with your therapist to discuss concerns, goals, and the patterns you notice in your relationship.

Sessions 2 & 3 - Individual Sessions

Each partner meets individually with the therapist. These sessions explore attachment patterns, significant life experiences, and emotional needs that influence your relationship.

Session 4 - Feedback and Planning

You return as a couple to review identified patterns, build shared understanding, and establish goals for your work together.

Sessions 5+

After the intake phase, sessions continue at a frequency that fits your needs and supports meaningful progress.
