Welcome to Aulakh Psychology

Compassionate care. Integrity. Trusting relationships.

We are a team of experienced Registered Psychologists offering compassionate, collaborative, and personalized care consistent with best practices. Together, we offer services to clients of various ages and developmental stages, and we work with children, adolescents, young adults, adults, parents and couples. We also work closely with individuals who work in, or have worked in, high risk settings such as veterans, first responders, and corrections officers.

Our team has extensive experience in working with clients with a range of mental health concerns and life stressors including: anxiety, obsessive compulsive disorder, stress, depression, adjustment to major life challenges, trauma, loss and grief, relationships concerns, coping with workplace difficulties and/or burnout, and chronic disabilities/illnesses.

We are known for our warm, empathic and collaborative approach. Using evidence based treatments, we work with our clients to address struggles, gain coping skills, manage identified challenges, and cultivate our clients' unique strengths. It is our pleasure and privilege to walk along side our clients as they move forward in a positive way in their lives.

Neurodiversity

Neurodiversity reflects the natural variation in how people think, learn, process information, and experience the world. Differences related to attention, sensory processing, communication, executive functioning, or social interaction are part of normal human diversity. However, when these differences lead to ongoing stress, burnout, self-doubt, or challenges at work, school, or in relationships, additional support can be helpful.

We can help you:
  • Identify strengths while addressing areas of challenge.
  • Develop practical tools for executive functioning, organization, and emotional regulation.
  • Navigate burnout, masking, and self-advocacy.
  • Strengthen relationships and communication.
  • Build confidence and self-acceptance.

Learn More

Mood Disorders

Experiencing shifts in mood is a normal part of being human - we all go trhough ups and downs from time to time. However, when these fluctuations become more frequent, intense, persistent, or overwhelming, they can begin to affect how we feel, think, and move through our daily lives.

We can help you:
  • Understand the patterns and triggers behind changes in your mood.
  • Learn practical tools to manage unhelpful thoughts and behaviors.
  • Build emotional resilience and coping strategies.
  • Reconnect with what matters most and engage in life in meaningful ways.

Learn More

Anxiety Disorders

Stressful events, uncertainty, and change can all trigger worry or nervousness. However, when anxiety becomes persistent, overwhelming, or begins to interfere with your work, relationships, or daily activities, it may be a sign that you need additional support.

We can help you:
  • Understand the root causes and patterns of your anxiety.
  • Identify triggers and physical responses.
  • Learn practical tools to manage racing thoughts and excessive worry.
  • Develop coping strategies to feel more grounded and in control.
  • Gradually face fears in a safe, supported way.
  • Re-engage in daily life with greater ease and confidence.

Learn More

Trauma

Trauma is the lasting emotional, psychological, and physical response to deeply distressing events that overwhelm an individual's ability to cope, such as violence, abuse, disasters, or severe loss. It shatters a person's sense of safety and self, often leading to mental health struggles like PTSD, anxiety, depression, and dissociation.

We can help you:
  • Establish physical and emotional safety, often the first step in healing so you will be safe in your own body and environment.
  • Learn grounding techniques, emotional regulation, and symptom management to stay within the "window of tolerance".
  • Safely navigate traumatic memories to reduce their power, often using techniques like EMDR, Cognitive Behavioural Therapy, or Cognitive Processing Therapy.
  • Develop a new sense of self that incorporates, but is not defined by, the trauma.
  • Restore trust in relationships and engage with your community.

Gender & Sexuality

Exploring and understanding your gender identity or sexual orientation is a deeply personal and meaningful process. For many people, this journey includes periods of reflection, growth, uncertainty, or change. While questioning or evolving in your identity is a normal part of human experience, feelings of confusion, isolation, fear of rejection, or distress can sometimes arise — particularly in environments that may not feel fully supportive or affirming.

We can help you:
  • Explore and better understand your gender identity and/or sexual orientation.
  • Navigate coming out and identity disclosure at your own pace.
  • Process experiences of stigma, discrimination, or minority stress.
  • Strengthen self-acceptance and confidence.
  • Improve communication and relationships.
  • Access resources and build supportive community connections.
